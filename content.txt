<h2>Best Central Coast Whale Watching Spots</h2>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The whales are about to start their journey past the Central Coast and grace our waters with their presence. From our many talented drone photographers to locals who love to catch glimpses of the whales, everyone is waiting with bated breath for their first sighting.&nbsp; We have heard that a few have been spotted already, so keep those beady eyes of yours peeled.</p><div class="code-block code-block-2" data-ai="WzIsMCwiSU4tTElORSBBUlRJQ0xFIC0gU1BPVCAxIExDQyBFdmVudHMgNiBtYXkgLSAxOSBtYXkiLCIiLDFd" style="margin: 8px auto; text-align: center; display: block; clear: both;">
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong><em>ADVERTISEMENT:</em></strong></p><p style="text-align: center;"><a href="https://www.lovecentralcoast.com/events" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone wp-image-13932" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/600x500_Inline-Article_Whats-On-1024x853.png" alt="Central Coast events" width="600" height="500"></a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><p style="text-align: center;">&nbsp;</p><p><a href="https://coastsheltersleepout.com.au/t/coasties-mag-team" target="_blank" rel="noopener">&nbsp;</a></p></div>

<p>As always we have done the hard yards and popped together this handy list of all of the very best of the Central Coast’s whale watching locations (as well as handy tips on where you can support local while grabbing a cuppa or a bite to eat).</p>
<p>Our region is set to welcome over 25,000 humpback whales to our waters during the migration months of May through November.&nbsp; It is no wonder that this mass movement of these majestic creatures has been termed the “Humpback Highway”.&nbsp; Travelling from Antarctica and towards the warmer waters of the Pacific, the whales glide through our region’s waters en mass playfully breeching and slapping their fins and tails, a truly wonderful sight to see in real life.&nbsp; Being on the Central Coast we are so lucky as our region boasts some truly outstanding vantage points, where even without binoculars, a drone or a fancy camera in hand you get the opportunity to see the whales up pretty close to shore.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<h3>Best places to&nbsp; spot whales on the Central Coast</h3>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<figure id="attachment_12022" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12022" style="width: 759px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" class="wp-image-12022 size-full" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/IMG_2088.jpg" alt="Whales central coast" width="759" height="1000" srcset="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/IMG_2088.jpg 759w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/IMG_2088-228x300.jpg 228w" sizes="(max-width: 759px) 100vw, 759px"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-12022" class="wp-caption-text">Image by @fromabove.images</figcaption></figure>
<h3>Best Whale Watching spots on the Central Coast</h3>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Crackneck Lookout, Wyrrabalong National Park</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<figure id="attachment_12120" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12120" style="width: 971px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img decoding="async" class="size-full wp-image-12120" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/image008-1.png" alt="Upgrade to Crackneck lookout great for Whale Watching" width="971" height="726" srcset="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/image008-1.png 971w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/image008-1-300x224.png 300w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/image008-1-768x574.png 768w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/image008-1-550x410.png 550w" sizes="(max-width: 971px) 100vw, 971px"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-12120" class="wp-caption-text">Upgrade to Crackneck lookout now complete – image supplied</figcaption></figure>
<p><a href="https://www.nationalparks.nsw.gov.au/things-to-do/lookouts/crackneck-point-lookout" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Crackneck Lookout</a> is the ultimate Central Coast whale watching spot, the views are uninterrupted!&nbsp; Newly renovated last year means that this spot has become only better to spend some time at.&nbsp; There are plenty of picnic tables or benches available to grab a spot to sit and watch while the kids can enjoy playing on the levelled-out tiers of grassed areas.&nbsp; Alternative for those feeling energetic you can walk the 3km trail and spot along the way at the various vantage points.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>During peak season and school holidays, this popular spot becomes busy and parking can be tricky, so our hot tip is to get there early. Grab a coffee from&nbsp;&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/bateaubeachcafe/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Bateau Beach Cafe</a> pack yourselves a picnic and spend the morning in the winter sunshine for hours enjoying the view.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Cromarty Hill, Forresters Beach</strong></p><div class="code-block code-block-1" data-ai="WzEsMCwiSU5MSU5FIEFSVElDTEUgLSBTcG90IDIgU2t5ZGl2ZSAyMyBBUHJpbCAyIHdlZWtzIiwiIiwxXQ==" style="margin: 8px auto; text-align: center; display: block; clear: both;">
<p style="text-align: center;"><em><strong>ADVERTISEMENT:</strong></em></p><p><a href="https://www.skydive.com.au/locations/sydney-newcastle/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><img loading="lazy" class="size-full wp-image-13878 aligncenter" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/SkydiveNewcastle_CoastiesAd_600x500px.jpg" alt="Skydive Central Coast " width="600" height="500"></a></p></div>

<p>Whether you’ve parked at Crackneck Lookout and walked over, or simply driven up to Cromarty Hill, you will not be disappointed by the views on offer. You can look south back towards Forresters Beach and Terrigal, or directly east across the vast ocean. There is limited parking available and a narrow, residential road, so be sure to take it easy and mind the walkers.</p>
<p><span class="WdYUQQ">Forries best coffee spots are </span><span class="WdYUQQ"><a href="http://dimatteos.com.au/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Di Matteos</a> or <a href="https://www.facebook.com/driftcoffeekazbah/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Drift Coffee Kazbah</a></span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>The Skillion, Terrigal</strong></p>
<figure id="attachment_12568" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12568" style="width: 1024px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-12568" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/fromabove.images_369178519_18009612775872142_4156587317966829470_n-1024x767.jpg" alt="Places to visit on the Central coast" width="1024" height="767" srcset="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/fromabove.images_369178519_18009612775872142_4156587317966829470_n-1024x767.jpg 1024w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/fromabove.images_369178519_18009612775872142_4156587317966829470_n-300x225.jpg 300w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/fromabove.images_369178519_18009612775872142_4156587317966829470_n-768x575.jpg 768w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/fromabove.images_369178519_18009612775872142_4156587317966829470_n.jpg 1440w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-12568" class="wp-caption-text">The Skillion in Terrigal by From Above Images by Christopher</figcaption></figure>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Skillion and the whole headland in Terrigal offer many great locations to view the whales from.&nbsp; Often we find the small car park between the two big headlands opposite the rugby club is an epic spot.&nbsp; There is a grassed area where you can set up a picnic or you can also head down onto the rocks closer to the water.&nbsp; Please note: this area is not patrolled so you need to be cautious and safe, especially if the weather is wet and the swell large.</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>Another crowd-pleaser for whale watching in Terrigal is The Skillion, this is the steepest hill found at Terrigal Haven. If there are whales in the area, you’ll definitely see them from up here. Plus there are many cool cafes for a coffee or breakfast stop after all that walking. Plus, views, views and more views for days from so many spots around the Haven.</p><div class="code-block code-block-8" data-ai="WzgsMCwiQmxvY2sgOCBTcG90IDMgY2NhcmQgZW5kIGphbiIsIiIsMV0=" style="margin: 8px 0; clear: both;">
<p style="text-align: center;"><strong><em>ADVERTISEMENT:</em></strong></p><p style="text-align: center; margin-bottom: 40px;"><a href="https://coastiesmag.com.au/subscription-plan/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><img loading="lazy" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-12569" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/Inline-display-ad-1.png" alt="Coasties Card App" width="600" height="500"></a></p></div>

<p>Our suggestions for caffeine: <a href="https://terrigalpavilion.com.au/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Terrigal Pav Pop Up</a> (note the Pavilion’s renovations are due to start in June 2024 so it is just the pop up window that will remain open during this time. Or <span class="WdYUQQ">the&nbsp;<a href="https://www.facebook.com/HavenBeachRestaurantandKiosk/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Haven Kiosk, </a></span>great coffee and even better seafood.</p>

<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Captain Cook Lookout, Copacabana Beach</strong></p>
<p>While it’s a bit of a trek to Captain Cook Lookout, it’s one of the best vantage points to watch whales on the Central Coast. Hugely popular year round, here you’ll be able to look out to the ocean that runs all the way to Terrigal, through Avoca and down to Macmasters. In other words, whale spotting is just about guaranteed.&nbsp; *Please note, “almost”, whales are creatures out in the wild, we of course cannot guarantee they will show up when you show up to see them!</p>
<p><strong>Coffee</strong>:&nbsp;<a href="https://ohblesshome.com.au/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Oh Bless Home</a>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<a href="https://www.instagram.com/thebeeskneeseatery/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The Bees Knees Eatery</a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Norah Head Lighthouse, Norah Head</strong></p>
<figure id="attachment_14047" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-14047" style="width: 819px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="size-large wp-image-14047" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/clay.sweetman_1696581834_3207483357885429721_8454454067-_1_-819x1024.jpg" alt="Whale watching central coast" width="819" height="1024" srcset="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/clay.sweetman_1696581834_3207483357885429721_8454454067-_1_-819x1024.jpg 819w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/clay.sweetman_1696581834_3207483357885429721_8454454067-_1_-240x300.jpg 240w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/clay.sweetman_1696581834_3207483357885429721_8454454067-_1_-768x960.jpg 768w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/clay.sweetman_1696581834_3207483357885429721_8454454067-_1_-1229x1536.jpg 1229w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/06/clay.sweetman_1696581834_3207483357885429721_8454454067-_1_.jpg 1440w" sizes="(max-width: 819px) 100vw, 819px"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-14047" class="wp-caption-text">Image by Clay Sweetman</figcaption></figure>
<p>Whale season or not, <a href="https://norahheadlighthouse.com.au/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Norah Head Lighthouse</a> is an iconic Central Coast landmark that is always worth a visit. You can park at the lighthouse or if you prefer a bush walk, park at Norah Head General Store. You walk through the bush to the lighthouse and then down the stairs to the rock shelf where you can loop back around to the café via the beach. Takes less than an hour in total, and you’ll undoubtedly see whales. It is also it’s a prime spot for spotting pods of playful dolphins.</p>
<p class="_04xlpA direction-ltr align-start para-style-body"><span class="WdYUQQ"><strong>Coffee</strong>: </span><a href="https://www.facebook.com/thegeneralstorenh/" target="_blank" rel="noopener"><span class="WdYUQQ">Norah Head Beach Haus</span></a></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Bouddi Coastal Walk, Bouddi National Park </strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="alignnone size-large wp-image-11879" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bouddi-1024x683.jpg" alt="bouddi coastal walk best places to whale watch central coast" width="1024" height="683" srcset="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bouddi-1024x683.jpg 1024w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bouddi-300x200.jpg 300w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bouddi-768x512.jpg 768w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bouddi-100x66.jpg 100w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2022/05/bouddi.jpg 1504w" sizes="(max-width: 1024px) 100vw, 1024px"></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p>The Bouddi Coastal Walk in <a href="https://www.nationalparks.nsw.gov.au/visit-a-park/parks/bouddi-national-park" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Bouddi National Park</a> runs from Putty Beach to Macmasters Beach, with a large part of the 8.km boardwalk offering outstanding ocean views and ample opportunity to spot whales. Take a picnic with you and enjoy at any of the many beaches you pass along the way. A wintery swim at one of the many beautiful spots along the way should also be part of your plan.</p>
<p><strong><span class="WdYUQQ">Coffee</span></strong><span class="WdYUQQ">: Hardy’s Bistro or <a href="https://www.thefatgoose.com.au/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">The Fat Goose</a></span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<figure id="attachment_12021" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-12021" style="width: 640px" class="wp-caption alignnone"><img loading="lazy" decoding="async" class="wp-image-12021 size-full" src="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res.jpg" alt="Whale Watching Spots Central Coast" width="640" height="640" srcset="https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res.jpg 640w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res-300x300.jpg 300w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res-150x150.jpg 150w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res-100x100.jpg 100w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res-600x600.jpg 600w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res-32x32.jpg 32w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res-64x64.jpg 64w, https://coastiesmag.com.au/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/med_res-260x260.jpg 260w" sizes="(max-width: 640px) 100vw, 640px"><figcaption id="caption-attachment-12021" class="wp-caption-text">Image by @zeemerven</figcaption></figure>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Bateau Bay Beach Viewing Area</strong></p>
<p>This sandstone viewing platform over Bateau Bay beach is a prime spot for whale watching on the Central Coast, even if it’s not as elevated as the rest. Best of all, there’s plenty of parking, great coffee at the B<a href="https://www.facebook.com/bateaubeachcafe/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">ateau Beach Café</a> and picnic tables throughout. If you enjoy a bushwalk you can walk north towards Shelley Beach, or south up to Crackneck Lookout for an even more spectacular view.</p>
<p><span class="WdYUQQ"><strong>Coffee</strong>: </span><span class="WdYUQQ"><a href="https://www.facebook.com/profile.php?id=100063034669430" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Cafe Mocha</a></span></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Toowoon Bay Beach</strong></p>
<p>If you’re walking or cycling the Coast to Lake walk as you pass through Toowoon we suggest you make a detour on Toowoon Bay Rd, at the end toward the water there are some steps down to the water and a couple of park benches. You’ll often be able to spot the whales from here as well as if you head down to the beach to take a dip in the ocean.</p>
<p>Pick up a breaky roll and coffee at <a href="https://www.instagram.com/flourandco_/?hl=en" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Flour &amp; Co</a> at the Toowoon shops or down on the beach at the <a href="https://www.facebook.com/VillageKiosk/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Village Kiosk</a> on the beach.</p>

<h2>Whale watching tours on the Central Coast</h2>
<p>Prefer to go on a whale watching tour? We don’t blame you!&nbsp; For those who have great sea legs, this is your chance to get even more up close and personal with these gorgeous creatures. Plus you get to learn a stack when on a guided tour and given that most vantage points require you to walk up a hill you get to save those pins from the burn!</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Terrigal Ocean Tours</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>

<p>The most popular whale watching tour on the Central Coast is <a href="https://terrigaltours.com.au/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Terrigal Ocean Tours</a>. Whale watching tours with Terrigal Ocean Tours run from May through to November. They depart Terrigal and sail around the Central Coast waters to find whales, with a 60 minute trip costing $70 at weekends and $60 midweek. They also accept Discover &amp; Parents NSW Vouchers.</p>

<h3><strong>Why do Whales migrate?</strong></h3>
<p>Around 25,000 humpback whales travel 5,000 kilometres from their summer feeding grounds near Antarctica to the warmer waters off Queensland and the Coral Sea to mate and give birth.</p>
<h3><strong>What types of whales can you expect to see on the Central Coast?</strong></h3>
<p>While humpback whales are the most spotted breed, the Central Coast has also been the sighting ground for southern right whales, minke whales and on the rare occasion there have even been orcas spotted in the waters off the Central Coast.</p>
<h3><strong>Will I spot a whale?</strong></h3>
<p>The migration season brings many chances for whale spotting off our coast, with the months of June &amp; July often renowned for some of the busiest month for the Central Coast spotting enthusiasts. If you’re going to spot a whale, this is the time. With 25,000 humpback whales migrating north along Australia’s East Coast there’s a good chance you’ll spot a whale off the Central Coast headlands.</p>
<h3><strong>When is the best time of day to see a whale?</strong></h3>
<p>You can spot a whale on the Central Coast at any time of day, but the morning time is usually the best due to the sunrise lighting illuminating their whale blow more clearly.</p>
<h3><strong>What to look out for when whale spotting on the Central Coast?</strong></h3>
<p>When looking out across the vast oceans, scan the water for imperfections and interruptions to the water’s surface. This could be an indication of a majestic whale just below the surface.</p>
<p>However, the most magical way to spot a whale is most certainly the glorious breach. This is when a whale propels two thirds of their body out of the water, landing with a ginormous splash. On the Central Coast, the whales appear to enjoy breaching often.</p>
<p>You can also look out for a blow, a thick spout of water shooting out from the ocean. Each variety of whale has a different blow which helps marine experts identify which species of whale they are.</p>
